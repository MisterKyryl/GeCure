Our team

Dr. Iryna Ruda

Iryna finished the Ph.D. program at the Department of Enterprise Economics at IFNUL and successfully defended her PhD thesis in economics and management in 2017. Currently, she is an Associate Professor at the Faculty of Finance and Business Management at IFNUL. Iryna has attended numerous workshops and courses aimed at improving her skill set in management. She has a record of management positions in various biomedical companies, possessing experience in managing hospitals with a staff of 100 to 700 people. Her professional interests include the design and development of new medical centers, new approaches for efficient management of medical centers, the design and standardization of processes and routes, as well as quality assessment and control. She is also actively involved in the design of teaching courses for master's and bachelor's students, including project management, marketing analytics, and macro- and microeconomic analysis.

Dr. Oleksandr Yushchuk

After being a PhD student in Ivan Franko national university of Lviv (IFNUL), Oleksandr successfully defended his PhD degree in molecular genetics in 2017. The thesis investigated the biosynthetic mechanisms of glycopeptide antibiotic teicoplanin. In 2023 Oleksandr successfully defended his DSc degree in molecular genetics, presenting works studying the regulatory and biosynthetic aspects of antibiotic production. Throughout his carrier, Oleksandr worked in several European universities. Currently Oleksandr is an Alexander von Humboldt research fellow at the department of genetics and biotechnology, IFNUL. He is experienced with different methods of bacterial genetics, general microbiology, molecular biology of nucleic acids, and analytical chemistry of natural compounds, and familiar with broad range of general bioinformatics tools. His research interests include searching for novel antibiotics and bacterial species, in silico and experimental investigation of evolution and spread of antibiotics resistance genes, as well as research on antibiotics biosynthesis.

Dr. Lilya Lehka

Lilya received her PhD at the Institute of Cell Biology, National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine, Lviv, studying the anticancer properties of angucycline antibiotics of landomycin family. Throughout her carrier, Lilya visited several European universities for mid-term internships and worked in industry. Today, Lilya works at the Laboratory of Molecular Basis of Cell Motility, Nencki Institute of Experimental Biology, Polish Academy of Sciences, Warsaw, Poland, currently holding a position of adjunct professor. At this point, Lilya works on the project studying the properties of unconventional myosin VI as a PI. Lilya is skilled in eukaryotic cellular biology, in vivo biology with murine models, and analytical cytology, histology, and biochemistry. Her research interests include investigating the molecular basis of cell motility, carcinogenesis, studying the molecular mechanisms of activity for novel anticancer compounds.
